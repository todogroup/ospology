# Open Source in Business Working Group Charter

## Mission

The Open Source in Business Working Group (OSS-in-Business WG) exists to fill a gap in the way organisations approach open source. While most guidance on open source is written from an engineering perspective, there is far less material that explains how it creates business value. The TODO Group launched this initiative through its Touchpoint calls to develop a collaborative guide that helps organisations understand how open source can drive business value.

The mission of the working group is to create a community-built reference that describes when and how open source should be incorporated into corporate strategies and business models, and to outline best practices for building sustainable open source ecosystems for business leaders.

This work will first focus on developing a Community Edition, which serves as the primary collaborative knowledge base produced by the working group. A future Enterprise Edition may be developed later as a derivative resource based on the Community Edition.

## Vision

We envision a future where business leaders across all industries, including non-software-native sectors such as hardware or manufacturing, treat open source as a strategic tool rather than a fringe engineering practice. Organisations will understand how to integrate open source into their core strategies, how to evaluate it alongside proprietary alternatives, and when it is or is not the right tool. The working group aims to increase the demand for open source initiatives as a business enabler.

This vision applies across both the Community Edition and any future Enterprise Edition, while recognising that each edition may serve different audiences and levels of depth.

## Scope and Objectives

The working group will first focus on producing a collaborative whitepaper from the Community Edition that addresses the following objectives:

- **Understanding how open source can drive business:** Provide business decision-makers with an understanding of, and framework for, how to integrate open source into the development and improvement of business models.
- **Guidance for non-software-native sectors:** Help companies whose core business is not software understand how open source can support and even transform their business models. This objective includes addressing concerns about vendor lock-in and ecosystem dependencies.
- **Case studies and success stories:** Compile case studies, success stories, and blueprints showing how open source strategies drive innovation and financial value.
- **Community outreach and partnerships:** Identify and engage partners beyond the open source community to co-publish or endorse the guide.

The group may later support the creation of an **Enterprise Edition** derived from the Community Edition. This edition is expected to present selected concepts in a business-manager-friendly format for business leaders and executives. The exact structure and format of the Enterprise Edition will be determined separately once the Community Edition has reached a first stable version.

### Clarification on the Community Edition and Enterprise Edition

The Community Edition is the foundational deliverable of this working group and the main space for collaborative development, review, and knowledge sharing.The Enterprise Edition will build on the Community Edition as phase 2 of the workflow.

## Roles and Responsibilities

The working group organises participants into clearly defined roles. These roles are volunteer and community-driven.

### Participants

Contributors self-organise into topic-oriented subgroups based on their interests and expertise. They also ensure that contributions follow the outline and objectives and that collaborative documents are well maintained.

### Chairs

The working group must have chairs who set agendas, coordinate meetings, and facilitate consensus. Chairs must have the ability to synthesise contributions from participants, surface areas of alignment and disagreement, and help the group turn discussion into decisions and actionable next steps.

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Community Edition

1. Contributors provide content via pull requests to the Community Edition repository.
2. The group reviews content asynchronously through GitHub comments and synchronously during meetings when needed.
3. Chairs help synthesise feedback, identify areas of agreement or disagreement, and merge content into the main branch once sufficient consensus is reached.
4. When the Community Edition reaches sufficient maturity, the guide is published on the TODO website.
5. Content from the TODO website is converted into PDF. This task is assigned to LF staff for proofreading and design.

### Phase 2: Enterprise Edition

1. Once the Community Edition is sufficiently mature, the working group begins a second phase focused on producing an Enterprise Edition.
2. The Enterprise Edition will build on the Community Edition by adapting selected material for business managers and executives.
3. Final content is converted into PDF. This task is assigned to LF staff for proofreading and design.

## Meetings and Communication

### Bi-weekly meetings

The working group meets every two weeks on Fridays. A calendar entry listing upcoming meetings is maintained on the TODO Group website, and chairs send reminders via Slack and email.

### Agenda management

Chairs must prepare a structured agenda for each meeting and share it at least 48 hours in advance using a collaborative tool such as Google Docs or GitHub Discussions. The agenda should include topics for discussion, links to draft materials for review, and clear action items. Chairs ensure that agenda items are addressed during the meeting and that action items are assigned to participants for completion before the next call.

### Meeting etiquette

Working group meetings are work sessions, not general networking or social events. Participants are expected to come prepared, stay on topic, and contribute actively.

### Asynchronous communication

Between meetings, communication occurs in the TODO Slack channel `#wg-oss-in-business` and through GitHub issues and pull requests.

## Deliverables

The primary deliverable is a guide/whitepaper explaining the business value of open source. The whitepaper repository already contains a preliminary table of contents covering the final sections.

## Participation, Conduct, and Contributions

As with all TODO Group projects, working group meetings and collaboration spaces are governed by the TODO Group Code of Conduct. These meetings must not be used for sales, self-promotion, or the promotion of commercial services.

All content contributed to the guide repository will follow the TODO Group content licensing model and will be made available under the **CC BY 4.0** license. By contributing content, participants agree that their contributions may be reused, adapted, and redistributed under this license, with appropriate attribution.

Any derivative works or reused sections based on this guide must explicitly reference the original source and be properly attributed as **“TODO Group Open Source in Business Working Group contributors.”**

## Governance and Transparency

### Public documentation

All charter, workflow documents, and roadmaps will be stored in a public TODO Group repository for visibility across all active working groups.

### Inclusive participation

Participation is open to anyone interested, regardless of membership status. The TODO Group Code of Conduct and Chatham House Rule for respectful collaboration are mandatory.

## Agenda and Collaborative Tools

To ensure effective meetings and follow-through:

- **Prepare agendas in advance:** Chairs must publish agendas at least 48 hours before each meeting using a collaborative platform such as Google Docs, GitHub Discussions, or Framapad. The agenda should outline topics, provide links to draft documents, and assign leads for each item.
- **Capture decisions and action items:** During meetings, chairs record decisions, action items, and responsible parties in the agenda document or meeting minutes.
- **Track progress asynchronously:** Chairs and participants should use pull requests, issue trackers, and comment threads to monitor progress between meetings.
